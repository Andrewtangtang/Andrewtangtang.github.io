# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Two confirmed audiences, evaluated together rather than ranked: **graduate-admissions committees and potential faculty/lab collaborators**, who are judging whether Andrew's research direction and depth fit their program or lab, and **recruiters and industry hiring teams**, who are judging whether his systems-engineering work is real and shippable. Both arrive already primed by a resume, referral, or link — they're here to verify and go deeper, not to be introduced to who he is from zero.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Yun-Tang (Andrew) Chang that makes the case, in one visit, that he does rigorous systems research **and** ships engineering work real people use. Success is a visitor who leaves with a specific, memorable belief about that combination and is motivated to explore further — into Projects or Experience — or to reach out directly.

## Positioning

Research depth + engineering execution: he doesn't just study systems (Confidential VMs, secure networking, stateful streaming) in the abstract — he also ships production-grade artifacts (DuckDB extensions with real community downloads, measured throughput and latency wins) that are actually in use. That combination — not either half alone — is the claim every section should reinforce.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: explore the **Projects** or **Experience** section to see the work firsthand.
- Secondary CTA: browse the full **Experience** timeline (`/experience`) for career trajectory.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "He does deep systems research, and he also ships things that get used."
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a serious systems researcher — real lab affiliations at NTU's Secure System Lab and University of Toronto's Computer Engineering group; (2) he also ships adopted, measured engineering work — DuckDB extensions with real download counts, measured throughput/latency gains; (3) that combination is rare enough to be worth a closer look at Projects/Experience; (4) worth reaching out about a research collaboration or opportunity.
- Proof on hand: none formally collected yet (no testimonials, press, or logos). The site already surfaces some quantitative proof organically within the Projects and Experience content (download counts, throughput/latency figures) — revisit this section once more formal proof (recommendations, press) becomes available.

## Brand Personality

Calm, rigorous, warm ("沉靜、嚴謹、有溫度"). The existing direction — deep jade and copper on a warm paper ground, serif display type, generous whitespace, understated hover states — already captures this and should be preserved and refined rather than replaced. No specific external reference site; the brief is to keep sharpening this established voice, not to chase a different one.

## Anti-references

Explicitly not: generic SaaS/startup marketing-site energy (gradient hero blobs, identical icon-card grids, pill buttons everywhere), and the "AI-generated template" tell — small all-caps eyebrow labels and numbered section markers (01/02/03) applied reflexively to every section regardless of whether the content is actually a sequence. Where numbering is used, it should earn its place because the content is genuinely ordered, not because "portfolios do this."

## Design Principles

- **Substance over spectacle.** Real research affiliations and shipped, measured engineering results (download counts, throughput numbers) do the persuading. Visual restraint keeps them legible instead of competing with them.
- **One deliberate accent, not decoration.** The copper accent and serif display type carry the brand's warmth on their own — resist adding more colors, gradients, or ornamental effects to compensate for restraint.
- **Practice what you preach.** A systems engineer's portfolio should read as precise and considered, not as a templated marketing page — every layout and copy decision is itself evidence of the craft claim.
- **Depth on demand, not on the landing view.** The homepage stays curated and skimmable; full detail (the complete experience timeline, the full project grid) lives one deliberate click away via honest, labeled links.
- **Numbers earn their place.** Only number a section (01/02) when the content is a real, ordered sequence — not as reflexive scaffolding on every heading.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline WCAG AA: sufficient text/background contrast (jade/copper text on the warm paper ground should be checked, not assumed), legible type sizes, and correct mobile rendering. No specialized accessibility requirements beyond that, but any future motion/animation work should still respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
